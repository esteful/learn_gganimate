Proof Without Words with gganimate

I recently watched the 2 part workshop (part 1, part 2) on ggplot2 and extensions given by Thomas Lin Pedersen. First of, it was really nice of Thomas to give the close to 4 hour workshop for the benefit of the community. I personally learnt a lot from it. I wanted to try out gganimate extension that was covered during the workshop.

There are several resources on the web that show animations/illustrations of proofs of mathematical identities and theorems without words (or close to it). I wanted to take a few of those examples and use gganimate to recreate the illustration. While this exercise may not qualify anywhere close to “useful” or “productive”, it was definitely a super “fun” way for me to try out gganimate.

This example is taken from AoPS Online and the result is that sum of first \(n\) odd numbers equals \(n^2\). \[ 1 + 3 + 5 + \ldots + (2n - 1) = n^2 \] The animated version of the proof is show below (R code, html file)
